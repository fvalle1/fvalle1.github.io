Physics's Ph.D. student

I'm a Ph.D. student in Complex Systems for Life Sciences passionate about modeling and applying new methods and mathematics to real-world problems.
During high school, I was at the Swiss National Supercomputing Centre developing analysis on cosmic rays data I worked at CERN during my bachelor thesis, implementing a new framework to monitor revelators. I also attended the inverted Cern School of Computing.
At the Master's level, I worked on modeling TGCA's cancer data using network theory. I studied correlations between linguistics, genomics, and different fields of study.
I am currently working on data mining on cancer datasets as a Ph.D. student. As part of my training, I am developing and studying tools for Natural Language Processing and actively contributing to the open-source community.
I am Chief Technology Officer of Elemento Modular Cloud, a company that want to transform the clouds into The Cloud.
